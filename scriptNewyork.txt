New York City. 1913.
A schoolteacher named Margaret earns $800 a year.
That's not poverty. That's a respectable life. She rents a clean apartment in Brooklyn for $8 a month. She buys a pound of beef for 15 cents. She saves $40 at the end of the year — and she puts it in the bank.
Because that's what careful people do. They save. They trust the system. They plan.
On December 23rd of that same year, in Washington D.C.
